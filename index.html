Phase 1: Intent Building 10% of budget

Slate + Bouquet Value Promos on Meta & Google. Show platform variety, not a single title. Run as engagement ads — target frequency of 2 videos per user. Goal: build "shelf value" so users know Stage has more than the one movie they watched.

Phase 2: Conversion 90% of budget

Retarget warm audience with title-specific ads. Budget split 50-50:

  • 50% on Naate — currently driving lowest CAC, keep what's working
  • 50% on Content Intelligence titles — data-driven selection (explained below). Mix of bridge titles + Parveen's recommended slate

What is Content Intelligence?

We analysed 4.01M churned users' viewing history in Snowflake. For each user, we know their acquisition content (first thing they watched) and where they naturally migrated during subscription.

Some titles are "bridge titles" — content users organically discover after their acquisition title. These bridges correlate with higher engagement (more completions, more subscription cycles). Instead of re-acquiring users with the same title that brought them originally (they already watched it), we use bridge titles in ads.

Example: 863K users were acquired via punarjanam. Among those who explored, users who ended up on vidshi-bahu or kaand-2010 had 4.2-4.8 avg completions (vs 3.0 average). These are the natural "next watch" — use them in re-acquisition ads.

Product Tracks — Detail

Three tracks applied across cohorts. Freemium builds habit. Monetisation captures value. Coins boost SR.

Track 1: Freemium A1, B1 — Primary

"Watch for Free" section on home screen — personalised, not random. Always visible, not gated.

  • 2-3 full movies free — bridge titles based on acquisition content (punarjanam user → show vidshi-bahu, not punarjanam again)
  • Microdramas — 10 episodes free per series
  • All series — 1st episode free (hook them into wanting more)
  • Tambola + chatbot games (already built — adds stickiness beyond watching)
  • Selection-based onboarding — "What do you want to watch?" → genre/mood pick → curated free content. This is how they enter the free section.

After free consumption → monetisation or paywall kicks in.

Track 2: Monetisation Models A2 — Primary | A1/B1 — After free

For users who won't pay ₹199/month. Same price ballpark, different packaging. No mandate. No auto-renewal.

Additional Ideas (Stakeholder Input)

From stakeholder review.

Special Content Access

Exclusive early access or premium content for re-acquisition users as a comeback incentive. "Only for returning members."

TV Offers

"Activate via TV first & get Stage at ₹29." Use TV as a low-friction entry point for users who have smart TVs.

CLM + Bot Calls for Dormant Users

60% of churned users still have the app installed, but 70% of those never open it. Run push notifications via CleverTap + automated voice calls + WhatsApp messages for users with app installed but dormant. Bring them to the app — then product catches them.

Secondary Metric: LTV per Re-acquired User

= Blended 12-month revenue per user across all plans.

Currently ₹232.

Guards against gaming — if we lower prices to boost re-acq %, LTV will drop. Both must be tracked together.

The check: Re-acq % × 4.2M × LTV = total monthly cohort value. This should go up.

Context

4.2M churned paid subscribers. ~250 users/day re-subscribe = ~7,500/month.

12-month blended LTV = ₹232 (across ₹99, ₹199, ₹399, ₹699 plans, R1=35%, R2+=30%). Almost all value captured in first 4 months.

Total value of one month's cohort = 7,500 × ₹232 = ₹17.4L (earned over 12 months).
