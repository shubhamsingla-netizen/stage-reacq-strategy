Walkthrough · 6 of 10 · Phase 1 Wall

Why we paused paid spend

Great relative lift, but economics didn't clear

CAC too high for LTV profile

Even with 30% SR on Gold tier, the acquisition cost per re-sub didn't line up cleanly against LTV on a payback-period basis.

Renewal dates clustered badly

Re-acquired users' repayment cycles didn't spread evenly — cash inflow was bunched, so recovery on ad cost was slower than the model assumed.

The decision

Stopped broad paid spend. Kept the tiered-pricing product changes live (they were already winning). Rebuilt the marketing motion around cheaper, owned channels.

Phase 2 · Pivot to owned channels

Smaller budgets. Learn what moves the needle at near-zero cost.

Push Notifications Hard learning

Target: full 40L churned cohort. Reachability ~4%, delivery ~0.1%. Edgy content-led messaging to drive recall.

Result: 0 conversions.

WhatsApp Hard learning

Same playbook. 9K messages delivered.

Result: 2 conversions.

New Meta campaigns Partial win

Smaller budget, tighter targeting, new re-acq creatives. CAC ₹800 (lowered from Phase 1, still not great).

Combined pool (re-acq + re-activation) → ~₹2K CAC — too high.

AI calls Parked

Cohort built: 7,190 Saanwari users. Scripts ready (Randeep Hooda personalized + Neighborhood generic). Not yet dispatched — parked until re-activation is fixed first.

Walkthrough · 8 of 10 · What's Next

Next · Marketing

Stitch channels together. Stop broadcasting, start flowing.

🎯 Correct user segmentation Foundation

Before we flow, we need to segment users correctly so each channel targets the right cohort. Broadcast-to-all is what made Phase 2 PN/WA fail.

Segmentation layers we'll build:

  • Cycle count — 1-cycle vs 4+ cycle users (3.1× return rate gap)
  • Content completion — finished vs partial watchers
  • Recency of churn — last 30 days vs 6+ months ago
  • Dialect + acquisition content — tie messaging to what brought them in
  • Reachability — WA-opted vs app-installed vs web-only

Each segment gets its own PN/WA/AI-call workflow — not one-size-fits-all.

🔔 PN + AI Call workflow Channel stacking

PNs cost ₹0. The question is how to stack them into a flow that actually converts.

Draft plan:

  • Invite user via Special Access → unlocked landing
  • Get them to watch content
  • If they drop off → PN with movie passes to pull them back
  • Stack with AI call dispatch for high-intent cohorts
🌐 Web as a new channel

App-side PNs hit reachability ceilings (4%). Web channels — email, browser push, retargeting pixels — haven't been tested for re-acq. Opening this up.

📣 Different communication

Generic "come back" messaging is dead. Move to content-specific, dialect-aware, behavior-triggered messaging. Use content intelligence layer we already built (Punarjanam = 32.5% conversion bridge).

Walkthrough · 9 of 10 · What's Next

Next · Product

Unused levers. Re-order the funnel, add new payment shapes.

🔄 Trial + Subscription flow

Combined entry — show both options side-by-side at the paywall. Test if dual-anchor reduces friction vs trial-only.

⏮ Subscription first, then trial

Reverse the funnel. Pitch ₹199 sub first; if they drop, offer trial as fallback. Shifts perceived commitment order.

🎞 TVOD (pay-per-view)

Transactional payment — buy one movie, not a subscription. Lowest commitment re-entry. Validates intent before asking for recurring payment.

🎁 Free zone + coins carrot

Earn coins by watching free content → unlock more content → graduate to paid. Behavior-first engagement loop.

Walkthrough · 10 of 10 · Wrap

TL;DR

Three beats the rest of the org should remember

1 · Phase 1 proved the model

Gold Tier framework works (3× higher SR). Tiered subscription works (2× revenue per user). Re-acq % × LTV is at all-time high.

2 · Phase 1 economics didn't clear, so we pivoted

CAC too high, renewal cycles clustered. Paused paid spend. Pivoted to owned channels — but broadcast PN/WA alone don't convert a cold churned base.

3 · Next is workflows, not volume

Stitch PN + Special Access + AI calls into a flow. Open web as a new channel. Product experiments: trial-sub ordering, TVOD, free zone with coins. Goal: 1% re-acq rate, ₹22L monthly LTV.
