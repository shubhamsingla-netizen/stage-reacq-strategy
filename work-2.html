What we set out to do

The opportunity no one was owning

4.26M users paid Stage, watched content, and left. Nobody was touching them after churn — they were abandoned. We stepped in.

We ran a 5-layer churn analysis and carved out a Gold Tier of 1.9M users (44.6% of base) — users with 4+ content completions and multi-cycle subscription history. Same ad spend, 2.86× higher return rate.

Strategic Framework

Phase 1 — Paid performance push

Scale fast, learn economics
What we shipped
  • Content re-targeting. Watched Punarjanam? Showed them Maha Punarjanam.
  • Channels scaled. Meta App, Meta Web, Google App campaigns. Continuous A/B on creatives.
  • Launched tiered subscription — ₹199 monthly / ₹399 quarterly / ₹699 yearly. Quarterly acts as decoy → users pick yearly. 2× revenue per user vs single-plan paywall.
Ads + Pricing

What worked

Gold cohort SR: 30% vs general 10% — 3× higher subscription rate from the same spend.

On the combined metric of Re-acq % × LTV, we're at an all-time high. Tiered subscription pushed per-user revenue up materially — even with flat returner counts, revenue moved meaningfully.

Why we paused paid spend

CAC too high for the LTV profile. Renewal dates on re-acquired users clustered badly — repayment cycles weren't lining up fast enough to recover ad cost. We called a stop on the paid-heavy approach and rebuilt around lower-cost motions.

Phase 2 — Pivot to owned channels

Smaller budgets, tighter targeting, learn what moves the needle at near-zero cost

Push Notifications

Hard learning

Target: full 40L churned cohort. Reachability ~4%, delivery ~0.1%. Used edgy content-led messaging to drive recall.

Result: 0 conversions.

WhatsApp

Hard learning

Same playbook — content-led re-engagement. 9K messages delivered.

Result: 2 conversions.

New Meta campaigns

Partial win

Smaller budget, tighter targeting, new re-acq creatives. CAC ₹800 (lowered from Phase 1 levels, but still not great).

Combined pool (re-acq + re-activation) campaigns running at ~₹2K CAC — too high for our LTV.

AI calls

Parked

Cohort built — 7,190 Saanwari users. Scripts ready (Randeep Hooda personalized + Neighborhood generic). Not yet dispatched — parked until we fix the re-activation side first.

The lesson

Owned channels at scale don't convert a cold churned base without better personalization and flow. Broadcast-style messaging (PN, WA) hit reachability ceilings. Paid spend on combined pool is too expensive. We need a workflow that stitches channels together — not just more volume on any one of them.

What's next

Stitched workflows + product levers we haven't pulled yet
AI calls + PN workflow

PNs cost ₹0 to send. The question isn't "send more" — it's how do we stack them into a flow that actually converts?

Draft plan:

  • Invite user via Special Access (unlocked landing experience).
  • Get them to watch content.
  • If they drop off → PN with movie passes to pull them back.
  • Combine with AI call dispatch for high-intent cohorts.
Channel stacking
Product-side experiments
  • Trial with subscription — combined flow at entry
  • Subscription first, then trial — reverse the order
  • TVOD — pay-per-view / transactional model for content
  • Free zone + coins carrot — earn by watching, unlock more
Flow experiments

TL;DR for stakeholders

Phase 1 paid push proved the Gold Tier insight and the tiered subscription lever — Re-acq % × LTV is at all-time high. But CAC didn't clear LTV cleanly, so we paused broad paid spend.

Phase 2 owned-channel pivot taught us that broadcast PN/WA doesn't work alone. We're now building stitched workflows (PN + AI call + Special Access) and testing new product flows (TVOD, coins, trial-sub ordering) before re-scaling spend.
